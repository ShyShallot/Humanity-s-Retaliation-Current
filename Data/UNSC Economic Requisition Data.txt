 UNSC Economic Requisition Data
This sheet was designed by GrandMasterApple & Murphy

To indicate and inform the economic balance of the unsc economy during the insurrectionist war and covenant war. 
Within reasonable margins of what the Empire at War game engine can handle

We are designing this to provide a canon realistic feel to the universe and to pre-calculate unit upkeep costs and maintenance so the playerbase does not have to worry about such things.

Data points Needed to understand this Informational Document

UNSC PARIS CLASS is now arbitrarily stated to cost 20 Billion Dollars
19,700,000,000 in Materials and Construction
300,000,000 in Troop and Crew

These numbers are generated on 4 point basis

1. The cost per metric ton of a UNSC ship made specifically by the UNSC will thus further be 0.0015 in game credits or 15,000$ 

2. The average cost of an American soldier in 2010 was 400,000$
We have rounded this number up to 500,000$ for all unsc personnel meaning that every 20 men = 10 Million

3. If a UNSC ship has Shielding - Double its Base Cost 

4. If a ship was initially paid for by the UEG or Otherwise and was bought and refit by the UNSC after its initial construction, Reduce its cost by 70% and Double its Build Time

Every 1 EAW in game credit is = to 10 Million $
Every 100 EAW in game credits = 1 Billion $

Thus with these costs, the Paris becomes our reference point at 2000 in game Credits 

The UNSC faction will start with an income of 40,000 Credits a year or 400 Billion$

The UNSC Faction will be capped to 100,000 Credits or 1 Trillion $ a year until year 35  

Year 35 will Enable a Multiplier to the UNSC budget of 4x to indicate the start of the Human Covenant war where the UNSC Military budget exploded up to 10x by the end of the war.

This enables the UNSC player to Make up to 400,000 Credits total or 4 Trillion $

Planetary Economic Data Calculation

We will start the player with 40,000 Credits a year as their primary budget gained from their most important planets IE: Earth, Mars, Reach, Harmony, Arcadia, Tantalus

Building economic facilities will allow the player to reach 100,000 Credits a year 60,000 more than baseline.

The player is capable of controlling 22 Planets and 1 Asteroid field by year 35.

This means each planet can produce up to 2608 Additional Credits through buildable economic sources.

We have 6 Economic Facility options  Available to the player at this time
2x Titanium Mine per planet (44)
1x Deep core mine (10 Available Planets)
1x Trade Station per planet (23)
Ultra Rich Resorts (2 - Tantalus , Arcadia)
(Infinite) Phoenix’s 25 Credits Per + 0.01% Planetary Income

Titanium Mine income: T
Deep Core Mine income: D
Trade Station income: S
Ultra Rich Resort income: R
Phoenix income (per): P
44T+10D+23S+2R+8P=60,000

Titanium (Extremely Common) → baseline
Phoenix (Uncommon) → better than Titanium
Trade (Very Common) → better than Phoenix
Deep Core (Rare) → high
Resort (Extremely Rare) → very high

Starting Fleet Requirements

The starting fleet of the UNSC cannot cost more than 1.7 trillion $ in Upkeep
This is because the player makes 40,000 credits per year which is 20% of the entire UNSC budget in 2490

We have allowed the player to grow that number to 1 Trillion a year via economic sources (with manufacturing sacrifices) which is 1 trillion$

If we scale economic sources to pay 80% of their income to upkeep costs for the UNSC player this means that at 100,000 credits the player is paying an automatic Allocation of funds numbering 400,000 Credits putting Upkeep allotted budget to
4 Trillion$ with a maximum designated budget for the UNSC player of 5 Trillion prior to the Human Covenant War

At the start of the Human Covenant War we automatically do a 4x wartime multiplier as a conceptualization similar to the USA entering WW2

Making the Maximum amount in income allocated to the UNSC during the human covenant war with only human based planets under their control = to 20 Trillion$

Meaning the UNSC player scales from spending 1.66% (factoring in upkeep) of Humanity’s budget in 2490 to a possible maximum of 20% of Humanity’s budget at the start of 2526

The Maximum possible income for the UNSC in Galactic Conquest will be calculated after we have finalized the Covenant Galactic Economy Sheet.

Covenant Economic Requisition Data

The UNSC economy is fundamentally human, industrial, and budget‑limited.
The Covenant economy is post‑scarcity, theocratic, and resource‑allocation‑driven.
They are not two versions of the same system — they are different species of economy entirely.

The Covenant economy is not based on money.
It is based on centralized extraction quotas and religious hierarchy.
• No currency
The Covenant do not “buy” things.
They assign resources.
• Post‑scarcity for common metals
They mine entire moons and gas giants.
Titanium, cobalt, vanadium, nickel — trivial.
• Scarcity only exists for exotic elements
Osmium, iridium, and plasma‑reactive materials are the bottlenecks.
• Production is controlled by the Prophets
Shipbuilding, mining, and military expansion are dictated by religious authority, not economics.
• Industrial capacity is enormous
A single Covenant core world can outproduce the entire UNSC.
• Losses are acceptable
A CCS‑class battlecruiser is expensive in resources, but not in political cost.
They can replace ships far faster than humanity.
In short:  
The Covenant economy behaves like a hyper‑industrial, theocratic empire where resources are abundant but controlled by religious caste systems.

The Covenant have infinite materials, but finite processing throughput. 

Ships in this regard cost nothing but time

Shipyards are limited
Foundries are limited
Slipspace cores are limited
Sacred components are limited
Skilled Sangheili crews are limited
Prophet authorization is limited
